For recreational tennis players, time is the biggest constraint. Work, family, and other hobbies all compete for your time. But with the right approach, even 3 hours of practice per week can lead to steady improvement.

## Recommended Weekly Schedule

### Day 1: Technical Training (1 hour)

**Warm-up (10 minutes)**
- Light jogging
- Dynamic stretching
- Short-court mini tennis

**Main Practice (40 minutes)**
- Multi-ball drill: Forehand placement × 50 balls
- Multi-ball drill: Backhand placement × 50 balls
- Alternating forehand/backhand × 30 balls

**Cool-down (10 minutes)**
- Serve practice × 20 balls
- Stretching

### Day 2: Rally Practice (1 hour)

**Warm-up Rally (10 minutes)**
- Medium-paced rally to find rhythm

**Themed Practice (40 minutes)**
- Cross-court rally × 10 minutes
- Down-the-line rally × 10 minutes
- Full-court free rally × 20 minutes

**Match Simulation (10 minutes)**
- Play a tiebreaker

### Day 3: Specialty + Fitness (1 hour)

**Specialty Training (30 minutes)**
- Focus on one weakness
- Examples: serve, net volleys, overhead

**Fitness Training (30 minutes)**
- Suicide sprints × 5 sets
- Side shuffles × 3 sets
- Jump rope × 500 reps

## Tips for Practice Efficiency

1. **Practice with purpose** - Know what you want to improve before each session
2. **Quality over quantity** - 50 quality shots beat 200 careless ones
3. **Video analysis** - Occasionally record yourself and compare to pros
4. **Find the right practice partner** - Someone at or slightly above your level helps most

## Summary

Stick with this plan for 3 months and you'll see noticeable improvement. Remember, progress isn't linear—you'll hit plateaus, but if you persist, breakthroughs will come.
